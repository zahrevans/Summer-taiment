About

"Berserk," created by Kentaro Miura, is a dark fantasy manga that follows the tumultuous journey of Guts, a lone mercenary known as the Black Swordsman. Set in a grim medieval world, the story explores themes of ambition, betrayal, and the struggle against fate. The narrative is rich with intricate world-building, showcasing a society riddled with war, political intrigue, and supernatural horrors. The artwork is renowned for its detail and intensity, capturing both the brutality of combat and the emotional weight of the characters' journeys. "Berserk" delves into the complexities of human nature, making it a powerful and haunting exploration of survival and the quest for meaning amidst despair.

About The Author

Kentaro Miura was a renowned Japanese manga artist best known for creating the influential dark fantasy series "Berserk." Born on July 11, 1966, he began his career in manga at a young age and published "Berserk" in 1989. The series gained critical acclaim for its intricate storytelling, complex characters, and stunning artwork. Miura’s work is characterized by its exploration of themes such as ambition, betrayal, and the human condition, set against a richly detailed medieval backdrop. Tragically, Miura passed away in May 2021, leaving behind a legacy that has profoundly impacted the manga industry and inspired countless artists and fans worldwide.
